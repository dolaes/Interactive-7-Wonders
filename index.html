Great wall of china

Great Wall of China

Huairou, Beijing, China

700 BCE

Length claimed to be 13,170 miles long Creation began in the 7th century BCE and took over two millennia to finish construction

Fun Facts

  • Best Time To Visit: September to October
  • Nearby Attractions: The Forbidden City, Temple of Heaven, Tiananmen Square, Summer Palace, and more.
  • Other Things To Do In Beijing: Explore Beihai Park, go shopping in Wangfujing, hiking in Mutianyu, and more.

Petra

Petra

Ma'an, Jordan

312 BCE

Noted carvers, the Nabataeans chiseled dwellings, temples, and tombs into the sandstone, which changed color with the shifting sun. A major earthquake in 363 CE caused more difficulty, and after another tremor hit in 551, Petra was gradually abandoned

Fun Facts

  • Best Time To Visit: March to May
  • Nearby Attractions: Dead Sea, Wadi Rum, Mount Nebo, Roman Theater, Ajloun Castle, and more.
  • Other Things To Do In Jordan: Visit the Jordan River, hike the Dana Biosphere Reserve, explore the Jordan Museum, and more.

The Colosseum

Colosseum

Rome, Italy

80 CE

The Colosseum in Rome was built in the first century by order of the Emperor Vespasian. Perhaps most notable were gladiator fights, though men battling animals was also common.

Fun Facts

  • Best Time To Visit: October to April
  • Nearby Attractions: Pantheon Temple, Roman Forum, Trevi Fountain, St. Peter’s Basilica, Vatican Museums, and more.
  • Other Things To Do In Rome:Explore the nightlife at Piazza Navona, admire Michelangelo’s paintings at Sistine Chapel, explore the Palatine Hill, and more.

Chichen Itza

Chichen Itza

Yucatan, Mexico

600 CE

Structure contains a total of 365 steps Among the most notable is the stepped pyramid El Castillo (“The Castle”), which rises 79 feet (24 meters) above the Main Plaza.

Fun Facts

  • Best Time To Visit: December to March
  • Nearby Attractions: Kabah, Pyramid of the Magician, Ik Kil, St. Ildephonsus Cathedral, Ek Balam, and more.
  • Other Things To Do In Yucatan: Caving at Cenote Zaci, exploring the Sacred Cenote, visiting the Sayil, adoring the charm of Museo Maya, and more.

The Taj mahal

Taj Mahal

Agra, India

1643 CE

The Taj Mahal represents the finest and most sophisticated example of indo-islamic architecture. Construction behan in 1632 AD, on the south bank of the Yamuna river in Agra, and was completed by 1648AD.

Fun Facts

  • Best Time To Visit: November to March
  • Nearby Attractions: Tomb of I’timad-ud-Daulah, Mehtab Bagh, Jama Mosque, Agra Fort, and more.
  • Other Things To Do In Giza: Sightseeing in Agra, tour the Khas Mahal, shopping in Kinari Bazaar, visit the Jehangir’s Palace, and more.

Christ The Redeemer

Christ the Redeemer

Rio de Janeiro, Brazil

1931 CE

Christ the Redeemer was designed by Heitor da Silva Costa, Carlos Oswald, and Paul Landowski. The resulting monument stands 98 feet (30 meters) tall—not including its base, which is about 26 feet (8 meters) high—and its outstretched arms span 92 feet (28 meters).

Fun Facts

  • Best Time To Visit: December to March
  • Nearby Attractions: Copacabana Beach, Sugarloaf Mountain, Ipanema, Maracanã Stadium, and more.
  • Other Things to Do In Rio de Janeiro:Witness the Brazilian heritage at Santa Teresa, trek through Tijuca Forest – the largest urban forest, enjoy shopping in Leblon, and more.

Machu Picchu

Cuzco Region, Peru

1450 CE

Machu Picchu is one of the few major pre-Columbian ruins found nearly intact Machu Picchu was believed to be the home to the “Virgins of the Sun,” women who lived in convents under a vow of chastity. Others think that it was likely a pilgrimage site, while some believe it was a royal retreat, but scholars are still questioning its purpose.

Fun Facts

  • Best Time To Visit: April to October
  • Nearby Attractions: Sacred Valley, Colca Canyon, Saksaywaman, Islas Ballestas, and more.
  • Other Things To Do In Peru: Enjoy hiking and backpacking through Inca Trail, mountaineering at Huascarán mountains, hike through Cordillera Blanca, and more.
